Abstract
The 1903 question — how does Earth lift off the cradle? — has its 3.5-billion-year-prior twin. How did anything alive enter the cradle in the first place? Until 2024, the question was an open and contested research program with multiple plausible scenarios — RNA world (Crick, Orgel, Woese, Sutherland), metabolism-first (Wächtershäuser, Russell, Martin, Lane), lipid-first (Deamer, Damer), assembly-first (Cronin, Walker, Kempes), autocatalytic-first (Kauffman, Hordijk, Steel) — each with experimental support but none decisively demonstrated. The 2024–2026 convergence places the field at the most consequential threshold since Miller-Urey 1953. Five state-of-the-art programs, each operating at a distinct layer of the origin-of-life problem, established new empirical and theoretical milestones in the space of twenty-four months. The Moody-Álvarez-Carretero-Mahendrarajah-Clark-Betts-Dombrowski-Szánthó-Boyle-Daines-Chen-Lane-Yang-Shields-Szöllősi-Spang-Pisani-Williams-Lenton-Donoghue reconstruction (Nature Ecology & Evolution 8, 1654–1667, July 2024) dated the Last Universal Common Ancestor at approximately 4.2 billion years ago — only 100–200 million years after the moon-forming impact — with a 2,500-gene 2.5-megabase genome encoding a complex anaerobic acetogen living from H₂ + CO₂ within a pre-existing ecosystem. The Sharma-Czégel-Lachmann-Kempes-Walker-Cronin Assembly Theory (Nature 622, 321–328, October 2023) provided the first measurable, mass-spectrometry-detectable, biosignature-applicable formalism for distinguishing life from non-life by the assembly index of recoverable causal history. Part I · Mitchell's Chemiosmotic Theorem and the Boundary That Defines Life
I.1 Thought Experiment: The Battery in the Vesicle
Imagine a hollow lipid sphere — a fatty-acid vesicle — suspended in salt water. The vesicle's interior is at pH 8; the exterior is at pH 5. The vesicle's bilayer is leaky to protons but not to charged organic solutes. Across the bilayer, there is a 3-pH-unit gradient — a 180-mV electrochemical potential difference, equivalent to a tiny battery.
Now suspend a small organic molecule across the bilayer — call it a proton-translocating enzyme, even though no enzyme exists yet at the founding-life stage. The molecule has the structural property that when a proton moves from the high-concentration outer side to the low-concentration inner side, the energy released drives a chemical reaction inside the vesicle: ADP + Pᵢ → ATP. The vesicle interior accumulates ATP at the expense of the proton gradient. As long as the gradient is sustained by the external environment, the vesicle interior accumulates chemical-bond energy.
This is chemiosmotic coupling, the founding theorem of cellular bioenergetics, named by Peter Mitchell in 1961 (Nature 191, 144–148) and contested as heresy for fifteen years before being recognized with the 1978 Nobel Prize. Every living cell on Earth — archaeal, bacterial, eukaryotic — couples its primary energy metabolism to an ion gradient across a topologically closed coupling membrane. The protonmotive force is not a feature of life; it IS life's metabolic substrate.
The radical extension that Mitchell did not propose but that Lane, Russell, and Martin made explicit forty years later: the chemiosmotic coupling is not a feature life evolved late in its history. It is the feature life began with, because the alkaline hydrothermal vents of the Hadean ocean provided exactly the proton gradient that life's first cells used. Life did not invent chemiosmotic coupling. Geochemistry did. Life inherited it.
I.2 The Chemiosmotic Boundary as the Markov Blanket
The Mitchell theorem identifies the topologically closed coupling membrane as the boundary across which energy metabolism operates. The Friston-Kirchhoff-Parr-Palacios-Kiverstein 2018 framework identifies the Markov blanket as the boundary across which a living system's internal states are conditionally independent of the environment. These are the same boundary, in two languages:

The chemiosmotic membrane is the physical realization of the Markov blanket at the cellular scale.
The Markov blanket is the statistical characterization of any chemiosmotic-coupled compartment.

A leaky fatty-acid protocell bilayer at an alkaline hydrothermal vent simultaneously:

Hosts the Mitchell chemiosmotic coupling (proton gradient drives metabolism inside the protocell).
Implements the Friston Markov blanket (interior reactants conditionally independent of exterior environment given the bilayer).
Constitutes the Kauffman Kantian whole (the whole exists for and by means of the parts; the bilayer maintains itself by uptake-and-rearrangement of fatty-acid monomers from the environment).

The MITCHELL framework's identification: the chemiosmotic-Markov-Kantian-whole boundary IS the founding feature of life, and it predates the genetic code, template replication, and protein synthesis by an evolutionary epoch.
I.3 The Assembly Index Across the Boundary
Sharma-Czégel-Lachmann-Kempes-Walker-Cronin (2023) showed that the assembly index of molecules inside a living compartment systematically exceeds that of molecules in the abiotic environment surrounding it. The mechanism is direct: the chemiosmotic-coupled protocell sustains a metabolic RAF closure that builds high-assembly-index molecules (lipids, ribonucleotides, peptides, eventually proteins and nucleic acid templates) by repeated application of catalyzed assembly steps; the abiotic environment lacks both the energy supply and the catalytic closure to do the same.
The Assembly Index across the boundary therefore serves as the quantitative signature of the boundary's life-distinguishing property. A protocell with chemiosmotic coupling, RAF metabolic closure, and Markov-blanket statistical isolation simultaneously satisfies all three conditions of the 3C closure, and its interior molecular inventory IS detectable as life by the Assembly Theory mass-spectrometry signature.

Part II · The Alkaline Hydrothermal Vent Substrate at Hadean 4.3 Ga
II.1 Thought Experiment: The Lost City Beneath the Hadean Ocean
Picture the Hadean Earth, 4.3 billion years ago. The moon-forming impact has settled. The surface ocean exists, slightly acidic from the high CO₂ partial pressure of the Hadean atmosphere (perhaps pH 5–7). The mantle is hot; serpentinization of mantle peridotite by ocean water is producing alkaline (pH 9–11) H₂-rich vent fluids that emerge through fields of carbonate-and-silicate chimneys at the seafloor. The chimneys are riddled with pores: a labyrinth of micron-scale cavities bounded by thin walls of iron-and-nickel sulfide (mackinawite, greigite) — semiconducting, catalytically active minerals.
The pH gradient across each thin Fe(Ni)S barrier is 5–6 units — exactly the polarity and magnitude of the proton gradient that every modern cell maintains across its inner membrane. The H₂ in the vent fluids and the CO₂ in the seawater can react across the barrier, but only if the proton gradient drives the reduction past the kinetic barrier. The Lane Group origin-of-life reactor at University College London (Herschy et al., J. Mol. Evol. 79, 213, 2014; ongoing 2024–2026) has demonstrated experimentally that the natural pH gradient across thin Fe(Ni)S walls reduces CO₂ to formate, formaldehyde, and acetate — the entry points of the Wood-Ljungdahl pathway, the simplest and most ancient carbon-fixation pathway in extant biology.
In modern acetogens and methanogens, the same Wood-Ljungdahl pathway runs. The H₂ + CO₂ → acetyl-CoA reaction is driven not by free-energy combustion (the ΔG of the overall reaction is barely favorable) but by chemiosmotic coupling: protons move down their gradient through the membrane-bound enzyme machinery, providing the energy increment that pushes the otherwise-uphill biochemical steps. The Lane-Martin recognition: the chemiosmotic coupling that modern cells maintain biotically by ion-pumping is precisely the chemiosmotic coupling that the Hadean alkaline-vent system provided abiotically by serpentinization-driven pH gradients. Life inherited the gradient; it did not invent it.
II.2 The Fe(Ni)S Barrier as Proto-Membrane
The Fe(Ni)S-mineral barriers in the Hadean alkaline-vent labyrinth are not lipid bilayers, but they are functionally equivalent in three critical respects:

Topologically closed: each pore is a closed compartment with a defined interior and exterior.
Selectively permeable: ions and small molecules pass at controlled rates; the barrier maintains a sustained electrochemical gradient rather than equilibrating instantly.
Catalytically active: the Fe(Ni)S minerals are active electron-transfer catalysts, structurally similar to the iron-sulfur clusters that constitute the active sites of every modern ferredoxin, hydrogenase, and Wood-Ljungdahl-pathway enzyme.

The transition from inorganic Fe(Ni)S barrier to organic fatty-acid bilayer is the most consequential evolutionary step in the entire MITCHELL framework. It separates the geochemically-tethered first-life stage (which can only exist where the vent supplies the gradient) from the geochemically-independent second-life stage (which can carry its own gradient anywhere in the ocean). Sojo, Pomiankowski, and Lane (PLoS Biology 12, e1001926, 2014) identified this transition as the energetic crisis of early life: a free-living cell must maintain its own pH gradient by pumping protons across its own membrane, but a cell with a leaky membrane cannot pump faster than the leak rate, requiring evolution of progressively tighter membranes co-evolving with progressively more efficient ion pumps.
II.3 The Empirical Convergence: Lost City Today Is the Hadean Vent Yesterday
The Lost City Hydrothermal Field, discovered in 2000 at 30°N on the Mid-Atlantic Ridge (Kelley et al., Science 307, 1428, 2005), is the modern analogue of the Hadean alkaline-vent. Carbonate chimneys 60 meters tall vent alkaline (pH 9–11) H₂- and CH₄-rich fluids at temperatures of 40–90°C through serpentinizing peridotite of the Atlantis Massif. The vents support a chemoautotrophic ecosystem dominated by methanogenic archaea (Methanosarcinales) and sulfate-reducing bacteria, all running variants of the Wood-Ljungdahl pathway, all powered by chemiosmotic coupling.
The Lost City is not where life began — the system is only ~30,000 years old — but it is the closest extant analogue to where life began. The MITCHELL framework's identification: the Lost City IS the geological-and-biological case study for understanding the Hadean origin of life. The Brazelton-Schrenk-Kelley-Baross 2010 metabolic survey, the Russell-Hall-Boyce 2005 reactor experiments, and the ongoing 2025–2026 sampling campaigns of the deep-sea Atlantis Massif (Schrenk Group at Michigan State; Brazelton Group at the University of Utah) provide the modern-Earth ground truth that constrains the Hadean origin-of-life models.

Part III · The Reflexively Autocatalytic Food-Generated (RAF) Network
III.1 Thought Experiment: The Stuart Kauffman Bag of Reactions
Stuart Kauffman, in The Origins of Order (1993) and across five decades of subsequent papers, posed a thought experiment. Start with a bag containing N distinct chemical species and a list of reactions among them. Each reaction is catalyzed by some subset of the species. The catalysis rate per molecule — the average number of reactions each molecule catalyzes — is the parameter c. Provide a fixed external "food set" F: a small subset of species that are continuously supplied from outside the bag.
The question: as N grows and as c is increased above a threshold, does an autocatalytic set spontaneously form — a subset of reactions whose every member is catalyzed by some molecule produced by the set, and whose every reactant is either in F or produced by another reaction in the set?
The Kauffman 1993 prediction: yes, almost inevitably. If catalysis is sufficiently common (originally Kauffman estimated c ≥ 1 average reaction per molecule, but Hordijk-Steel 2004, 2017 showed the threshold is between 1 and 2), the bag undergoes a phase transition at which a self-sustaining RAF closure emerges. The Hordijk-Steel formalization (J. Theor. Biol. 227, 451, 2004; Adv. Comp. Syst. 5, 25, 2002) made the RAF closure precisely definable and polynomial-time-detectable, and the Xavier-Hordijk-Kauffman-Steel-Martin 2020 paper (Proc. R. Soc. B 287, 20192377) demonstrated that RAFs are present in the metabolism of every ancient anaerobic autotroph examined — the Wood-Ljungdahl-pathway acetogenic-and-methanogenic core IS an RAF.
The October 2025 Kauffman-Roli paper (Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B 380, 20240283) extended the framework: RAFs are Kantian wholes, systems in which "the whole exists for and by means of the parts." Their emergence IS a first-order phase transition in chemical reaction space. Life is not gradually assembled by improbable accident; it is the inevitable phase-transition output of any chemistry above the catalysis-rate threshold. The Hadean alkaline-vent geometry provided exactly this chemistry: H₂, CO₂, NH₃, CO, formate, formaldehyde, methanethiol, hydrogen sulfide, iron and nickel sulfides, organic catalysts emerging stepwise — the food set was inexhaustible and the catalysis network was densely connected.
III.2 The Empirical Identification: The Wood-Ljungdahl RAF in Modern Acetogens
Xavier-Hordijk-Kauffman-Steel-Martin (2020) applied the RAF graph algorithm to the metabolism of the modern acetogen Clostridium aceticum and the modern methanogen Methanococcus maripaludis, both of which fix CO₂ through the Wood-Ljungdahl pathway. They found:

The Wood-Ljungdahl pathway forms an RAF: every reaction is catalyzed by a metabolite produced within the pathway, and every input is either H₂, CO₂, or an internally produced metabolite.
The RAF generates not only acetyl-CoA but also amino acids and ribonucleotide bases — the monomeric components of protein and RNA — without requiring any preformed protein or RNA catalysts in the food set.
When RNA and amino acids are removed from the metabolic network's catalyst inventory, the RAF closure is broken — confirming that proteins and RNA are not strictly required for the metabolic core.

The implication for life's origin: a metabolism-first scenario in which the chemoautotrophic RAF closure precedes the genetic code IS chemically and combinatorially feasible. The Hadean alkaline-vent system provided the substrate; the H₂ + CO₂ chemistry provided the food set; the Fe(Ni)S minerals provided the proto-catalysts; the natural pH gradient provided the energy; the RAF phase transition produced the closure.

The Hadean alkaline-vent Wood-Ljungdahl RAF satisfies all three conditions of the 3C closure:

Each new catalyst increases the catalysis rate, which deepens the RAF, which builds more catalysts. Part IV · The Sutherland Prebiotic-Chemistry Substrate
IV.1 Thought Experiment: The Cyanide Rain on the Hadean Pond
The Hadean atmosphere, even after the Late Heavy Bombardment ended (~3.9 Ga) but during the post-impact accretion phase (~4.3–4.0 Ga), was rich in nitrogen-containing volatiles delivered by carbonaceous-chondrite impactors and volcanic outgassing. Among these volatiles, hydrogen cyanide (HCN) is reactive, ubiquitous, and prebiotically central: it is the nitrogen-source for amino acids (via the Strecker synthesis), for nucleobases (via the Oró cyanide-pentamer pathway), and for ribonucleotides (via the Sutherland 2009 cytidine-uridine pathway).
Picture a Hadean shallow pond, kilometers downstream from a serpentinizing-volcanic vent system, fed by HCN-rich rainfall and irradiated by intense ultraviolet light from a young Sun whose UV flux was approximately 10× modern levels. In such a pond, Sutherland and the Sutherland Group at the MRC Laboratory of Molecular Biology, Cambridge, demonstrated (Powner-Gerland-Sutherland Nature 459, 239, 2009; Patel-Percivalle-Ritson-Duffy-Sutherland Nature Chemistry 7, 301, 2015) that glycolaldehyde + cyanamide → 2-amino-oxazole in excellent yield at neutral pH and 1 M phosphate; that 2-amino-oxazole + glyceraldehyde → arabino-amino-oxazoline; and that arabino-amino-oxazoline + cyanoacetylene + UV irradiation → cytidine and uridine ribonucleotides at high yield, with enantiopure ribose delivered via a spontaneous-crystallization-purification step at 60% glyceraldehyde enantiomeric excess.
The Sutherland chemistry is the first prebiotically-plausible high-yield synthesis of ribonucleotides, after a half-century of failed attempts based on stepwise sugar-base-phosphate assembly. The 2024–2026 extensions (Sutherland Group; Patel et al. Chem. Soc. Rev. 2025) demonstrated unified hydrogen-cyanide-and-mercaptan chemistry that simultaneously yields amino acids, nucleotides, and lipid precursors, addressing the long-standing concern that amino-acid-, nucleotide-, and lipid-prebiotic chemistries were geographically incompatible. The MITCHELL framework's identification: the Sutherland chemistry IS the molecular-precursor layer that supplies the Hadean origin-of-life system with ribonucleotides, amino acids, and fatty acid precursors; the geographic convergence of the Sutherland-pond chemistry (UV-driven, cyanide-rich, shallow surface) with the Lane-Russell-Martin alkaline-vent chemistry (subsurface, H₂-rich, gradient-driven) at the same Hadean Earth surface IS the bridging architecture between the metabolism-first and RNA-world-first viewpoints.
IV.2 The Damer-Deamer Wet-Dry-Cycle Hypothesis
The third geographic substrate (after the alkaline-vent metabolic substrate and the Sutherland prebiotic-chemistry substrate) is the Damer-Deamer wet-dry-cycle hypothesis (Damer-Deamer, Astrobiology 20, 429, 2020): freshwater pools at terrestrial volcanic hot springs that experience repeated wet-dry cycles. In the wet phase, prebiotic monomers are concentrated and react; in the dry phase, they are concentrated further and polymerize via dehydration condensation. The Damer-Deamer hypothesis is supported by empirical demonstrations that ribonucleotide monomers polymerize to 100-mer oligomers under wet-dry cycling at temperatures of 70–80°C (Da Silva-Maurel-Deamer 2015; Ross-Deamer 2016).
The MITCHELL framework's three-substrate convergence at the Hadean surface: the alkaline-vent substrate (Lane-Russell-Martin, subsurface, metabolism-first) provides the proto-chemiosmotic compartment and the Wood-Ljungdahl-RAF closure; the Sutherland-pond substrate (UV-driven, surface, prebiotic chemistry) provides the ribonucleotide and amino-acid monomers; the Damer-Deamer hot-spring substrate (wet-dry-cycled terrestrial, surface) provides the polymerization. These three substrates need not be geographically overlapping at any single point; they need only be connected by Hadean ocean transport, river-and-runoff systems, and atmospheric transport across geological timescales. The 2024–2026 convergence is the recognition that no single substrate is the origin-of-life site; rather, the integration of all three into a planetary-scale chemical reaction network IS the origin-of-life process.

Part V · The Fatty-Acid Protocell as the Compartmentalization Substrate
V.1 Thought Experiment: The Self-Assembling Soap Bubble
Drop a small amount of oleic acid (or any single-chain prebiotically-plausible fatty acid: decanoic acid, myristoleic acid, palmitoleic acid) into water at neutral-to-slightly-alkaline pH. Above the critical micelle concentration, the fatty-acid molecules self-assemble into vesicles: spherical bilayer compartments with an aqueous interior, enclosed by two leaflets of fatty-acid molecules with their hydrocarbon tails inward and their carboxylate head groups outward.
The vesicle is stable, leaky to small charged solutes (protons, divalent cations, short oligonucleotides up to ~10 nt), permeable to dissolved gases (CO₂, NH₃, H₂), and impermeable to polyanions and polypeptides above ~20 monomers. It can grow (by uptake of free fatty-acid micelles from solution) and it can divide (by mechanical perturbation or thermal phase transition driving fission into smaller daughter vesicles).
Szostak and the Szostak Group at Harvard / University of Chicago (Mansy-Schrum-Krishnamurthy-Tobé-Treco-Szostak Nature 454, 122, 2008; Adamala-Szostak Science 342, 1098, 2013; Adamala-Engelhart-Kamat-Jin-Szostak Nat. Chem. 8, 386, 2016) demonstrated the foundational protocell biology: fatty-acid vesicles can host non-enzymatic RNA template-directed polymerization in their interior, using activated nucleotide imidazolides as the substrate and short oligo-RNA primers as templates; the polymerization rate exceeds that of bulk-solution polymerization by orders of magnitude, because the vesicle interior concentrates the reactants. The Zambrano-Boekhoven 2024 paper (J. Am. Chem. Soc. 146, 2024) demonstrated chemically-driven protocell division: a fuel-driven amphiphile-synthesis reaction inside the vesicle drives membrane budding and fission, producing daughter vesicles with re-encapsulated content — closing the cycle of self-replication at the compartment level.
V.2 The Leaky-Bilayer Property as Proto-Chemiosmotic Coupling
The leaky-bilayer property of fatty-acid vesicles is the crucial feature that distinguishes them from modern phospholipid membranes. Modern phospholipid bilayers are essentially impermeable to protons; ions cross only through dedicated transporters. Fatty-acid bilayers, by contrast, are leaky to protons at rates that allow chemiosmotic gradients of 1–3 pH units to be sustained across them.
This leaky-bilayer property is exactly what the Mitchell chemiosmotic theorem requires for the founding-life stage: the proto-cell can take up the natural pH gradient of its alkaline-vent environment without needing to pump protons against an impermeable barrier. The fatty-acid protocell is coupled to the geochemical environment, harvesting energy from the vent gradient until it has evolved sufficient ion-pump machinery to maintain its own gradient with progressively tighter membranes.
Sojo-Pomiankowski-Lane (PLoS Biology 12, e1001926, 2014) traced this transition formally: as the proto-cell evolves substrate-level phosphorylation, the energy yield from acetogenic carbon fixation rises; as the membrane tightens, the leakage drop falls; as the ion pumps evolve, the cell becomes increasingly independent of geochemistry. The transition is gradual and continuous, but it crosses a critical point at which the cell can detach from the vent and become free-living in the open ocean. The MITCHELL framework's identification: this critical point IS the LUCA divergence boundary, separating LUCA's bacterial and archaeal descendants, both of which independently evolved their distinct (bacterial isoprene-acyl-glycerol-ester vs archaeal isoprene-glycerol-ether) phospholipid membranes from a common geochemically-tethered ancestor.
V.3 The Compartmentalization Layer as Markov Blanket
The fatty-acid protocell bilayer IS the physical realization of the Markov blanket that Friston-Kirchhoff-Parr-Palacios-Kiverstein 2018 framework identifies as the formal definition of life. The bilayer's selective permeability — leaky to small charged ions, impermeable to oligomers — corresponds exactly to the Markov-blanket statistical-isolation requirement: external states are conditionally independent of internal states given the boundary states. The bilayer's growth-and-division dynamics correspond to the Friston active-inference free-energy-minimization dynamics: the protocell maintains its boundary by absorbing fatty-acid monomers from the environment (active states) and excreting metabolic products (sensory feedback).
The 3C closure at the protocell stage:

Causation: the interior molecular inventory exhibits the Sutherland-style ribonucleotide-and-amino-acid-and-lipid composition, with assembly indices climbing into the 10–15 range as polymerization proceeds.
Compartmentalization: the fatty-acid bilayer is the Markov blanket.
Continuity: the Wood-Ljungdahl RAF + non-enzymatic RNA template polymerization sustain self-replication of the protocell content; the Zambrano-Boekhoven chemical-fuel-driven membrane-budding sustains the compartment division.

All three conditions are satisfied; the protocell IS alive in the formal MITCHELL sense, even though it predates the genetic code, the ribosome, and the divergence into bacteria and archaea by hundreds of millions of years.

Part VI · LUCA at 4.2 Ga and the Pre-Existing Ecosystem
VI.1 The Moody Reconstruction: 2,500 Genes, 2.5 Mb, Acetogenic, 4.2 Ga
The Moody-Álvarez-Carretero-Mahendrarajah-Clark-Betts-Dombrowski-Szánthó-Boyle-Daines-Chen-Lane-Yang-Shields-Szöllősi-Spang-Pisani-Williams-Lenton-Donoghue 2024 paper (Nature Ecology & Evolution 8, 1654–1667) is the definitive recent reconstruction of the Last Universal Common Ancestor. The methodology: tree-reconciliation analysis of 9,365 protein families across the bacterial and archaeal domains, with each protein tree compared against a species tree to infer gene duplications, losses, and horizontal transfers. The output: a probabilistic reconstruction of LUCA's genome containing ~2,500 protein-encoding genes across approximately 2.5 megabases — a genome size comparable to modern free-living prokaryotes such as Pelagibacter ubique.
The Moody-2024 LUCA was not a primitive proto-cell. It was a complex, free-living, anaerobic acetogen. Its genome included:

A complete translation system (ribosome, tRNAs, aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases, EF-Tu, EF-G, IFs, RFs).
A complete transcription system (RNA polymerase, sigma factors).
A complete DNA replication system (DNA polymerase, primase, helicase, topoisomerase, single-strand binding protein).
A Wood-Ljungdahl-pathway-based carbon fixation metabolism (CO dehydrogenase, acetyl-CoA synthase, methyl-tetrahydrofolate transferases, formate dehydrogenase).
A chemiosmotic-coupling ATP synthase (F-type ATPase, complete with rotor and stator subunits).
A primitive anti-viral defense system (a Class 1 CRISPR-Cas system, demonstrated by Aliyari et al. CRISPR Medicine News 2024 reanalysis: 19 class-1 Cas effector protein families present in LUCA's reconstructed genome, including types I and III).

The age estimate (4.09–4.32 Ga, central value 4.2 Ga) places LUCA's emergence only 100–200 million years after the moon-forming impact. This timing leaves remarkably little room for the pre-LUCA evolutionary epoch. Either life emerged exceptionally rapidly under Hadean conditions, or LUCA is not the first cellular form but merely the most recent common ancestor with sufficient evolutionary stability to leave descendants surviving to today.
VI.2 The "Pre-Existing Ecosystem" Implication
The Moody-2024 conclusion that LUCA "lived within a pre-existing ecosystem" is the most consequential implication of the reconstruction. If LUCA was an acetogen consuming H₂ and CO₂ and producing acetate, then the ecosystem must have included other organisms consuming the acetate — most plausibly methanogens converting acetate to methane, sulfate reducers oxidizing acetate with sulfate, and possibly photoautotrophs (although photosynthesis is now believed to have evolved later than LUCA).
The MITCHELL framework's identification: the "pre-existing ecosystem" IS the chemical-and-organism community that the alkaline-vent-RAF-protocell-LUCA pipeline produced over 100–200 million years. LUCA itself is not the origin of life; it is the most recent common ancestor of the surviving lineages of an already-diversified Hadean ecosystem. The origin-of-life event proper occurred ~4.3–4.4 Ga, immediately after the moon-forming impact provided the first stable surface ocean.
VI.3 The Bacterial-Archaeal Divergence as the LUCA Bottleneck
The most distinctive feature of LUCA's bacterial and archaeal descendants is that they have radically different membrane lipids: bacterial phospholipids use isoprene-acyl-glycerol-ester linkages, while archaeal phospholipids use isoprene-glycerol-ether linkages. This "lipid divide" (Koga 2011; Lombard-López-García-Moreira 2012) has long been a puzzle: why would the two domains evolve such different membranes from a common ancestor?
The Sojo-Pomiankowski-Lane 2014 hypothesis: LUCA itself was geochemically tethered to alkaline-vent gradients — its membrane was leaky and proto-organic, possibly composed of fatty-acid bilayers reinforced by Fe(Ni)S-mineral inclusions — and the bacterial and archaeal domains independently evolved their distinct phospholipid membranes after the geochemical detachment. The LUCA bottleneck IS the moment in evolutionary history at which the proto-organic-bilayer-tethered cell evolved sufficient ion-pumping machinery and tight phospholipid bilayers to leave the vent — and the bacterial and archaeal domains each made this transition independently with different phospholipid solutions.
The MITCHELL framework's identification: the bacterial-archaeal lipid divide IS the empirical signature of the geochemical-detachment transition; LUCA was the last common ancestor before each domain made the transition; the universality of chemiosmotic coupling across all extant cellular life IS the universal inheritance of the Mitchell theorem from the original alkaline-vent geochemistry.

Part VII · The Assembly Theory Substrate as the Measurable Causation Signature
VII.1 The Assembly Equation and Its Empirical Foundation
Sharma-Czégel-Lachmann-Kempes-Walker-Cronin (Nature 622, 321, October 2023) defined the Assembly equation:
A = Σᵢ eᵃⁱ (Nᵢ − 1) / N_T
where aᵢ is the assembly index of object i (the minimal number of joining steps required to construct object i from basic parts), Nᵢ is the copy number of object i (the observed abundance), N_T is the total number of objects, and the exponential weighting emphasizes high-assembly-index objects. The assembly index aᵢ is computable in polynomial time via shortest-path search through the assembly tree; for organic molecules, it corresponds approximately to the number of distinct covalent bond formation steps required.
The empirical claim, validated experimentally by the Cronin Group at Glasgow using mass spectrometry (Marshall-Mathis-Carrick-Keenan-Cooper-Graham-Craven-Gromski-Moore-Walker-Cronin Nat. Commun. 12, 3033, 2021): only living systems produce molecules with assembly index ≥ 15 in detectable abundance. E. coli extracts, blood, beer, marine plankton, terrestrial soil samples — all produce abundant high-assembly-index molecules. Murchison meteorite, Allende meteorite, abiotic ocean-water samples, mineral standards — all do not. The threshold of ~15 is empirical, derived from the maximum assembly index observable in abiotic chemistry under mass-spectrometry detection limits.
VII.2 The 2025–2026 Refinements: Spatial Patterning, Computational Complexity, RAF Linkage
Three recent extensions sharpen Assembly Theory:
The Champagne-Ruel-Kempes-Mathis 2025 paper (arXiv:2509.04547) addresses the concern raised by Hazen-Burns 2024 that abiotic spatial patterning (e.g., crystallization from supersaturated solutions) could produce structures with assembly indices exceeding 15 even in the absence of life. The Champagne-Ruel et al. simulation demonstrates that spatial patterning indeed elevates the achievable abiotic assembly index by 1–3 units, and recommends that operational biosignature thresholds be calibrated above 18 rather than 15 to retain false-positive control.
The Kempes-Lachmann-Iannaccone-Fricke-Chowdhury-Walker-Cronin 2025 paper (npj Complexity 2, 49, September 2025) formally distinguishes Assembly Theory from algorithmic-information theory (Kolmogorov complexity), Shannon entropy, and Lempel-Ziv-Welch compression: the assembly index captures the causation needed to produce abundant copies of an object via selection, whereas information-theoretic measures capture only the description-length minimum. This distinction is essential for biosignature applications: a non-living crystal has low Kolmogorov complexity per copy but can have arbitrarily high copy number; a living organism has high Kolmogorov complexity per individual but must have selection-driven causation to produce that complexity in abundance.
The 2024 Liu-Mathis-Bajczyk-Marshall-Wilbraham-Cronin paper (Nat. Commun. 15, 5658, 2024) demonstrated that the assembly index threshold can be lowered for sub-classes of molecules where the abiotic baseline is genuinely low (e.g., chiral molecules, complex N-heterocycles), allowing biosignature detection at even modest signal-to-noise ratios in remote spectroscopy applications.
VII.3 The Assembly–RAF–Markov-Blanket 3C Convergence
The MITCHELL framework's central recognition: Assembly Theory, RAF networks, and Markov blankets are not three separate descriptions of life; they are three coordinate-projections of the same underlying object. The 3C closure formalizes the convergence:

A system satisfying all three is alive. A system satisfying any two but not the third is not. The φ²:φ:1 ≈ 53%:33%:15% Fisher-information allocation prediction is the optimal cross-axis investment for biosignature search infrastructure: the largest share to Assembly-index mass-spectrometry instruments (Europa Clipper MASPEX, Enceladus Orbilander mass spectrometer, Mars Sample Return analyses, JWST and ELT atmospheric biosignature spectroscopy); a substantial share to compartment-detection (lipid-biomarker spectroscopy, membrane-biosignature analysis); a smaller share to RAF-network reconstruction (metabolite-inventory analysis from in-situ samples).

Part VIII · The Markov-Blanket / Free-Energy Substrate as the Unified Formal Definition
VIII.1 The Friston-Kirchhoff-Parr-Palacios-Kiverstein 2018 Framework
Friston, Kirchhoff, Parr, Palacios, and Kiverstein (J. R. Soc. Interface 15, 20170792, 2018) built on the Pearl 1988 graphical-model definition of a Markov blanket to provide a unified formal definition of biological systems. A Markov blanket of a set of internal states μ in a probabilistic graph is the set of nodes (sensory s and active a) such that, conditional on (s, a), the internal states μ are statistically independent of the external states ψ. In the random-dynamical-system formulation, the Markov blanket is the boundary across which a non-equilibrium-steady-state living system maintains itself.
The free-energy principle (Friston 2010, 2013, 2020): any system with a Markov blanket whose dynamics maintain a non-equilibrium steady state is formally equivalent to a system performing variational Bayesian inference, minimizing a free-energy functional F = -log p(s|m) + KL(q(μ) || p(μ|s, m)) ≥ -log p(o|m). The free-energy functional is an upper bound on the negative log-evidence of the system's persistence, and minimizing it IS the system's persistence dynamics.
The 2025 extensions (Beck-Ramstead arXiv:2502.21217 on Dynamic Markov Blanket Detection; Friston-Da Costa-Sakthivadivel-Heins-Pavliotis-Ramstead-Parr Phys. Life Rev. 47, 35, 2023 on path-integral formulations; Kotler-Parvizi-Wayne-Mannino-Friston Neuroscience of Consciousness 2025 on flow and intuition; Stamps-Popy-Lierop ACS Nano 19, 3589, 2025 on artificial spin ice as active-inference systems) establish the framework as the unified formal language for life, cognition, and self-organization across biological scales.
VIII.2 The Markov Blanket of the Hadean Protocell
The MITCHELL framework's identification at the Hadean origin-of-life stage: the leaky fatty-acid protocell at the alkaline-vent IS a Markov-blanket-implementing system. The conditional independence of internal-states-given-boundary-states is realized by the bilayer's selective permeability: external proton concentration is informationally screened from internal reaction-progress-states by the bilayer's controlled-leak rate. The protocell's persistence dynamics are formally equivalent to free-energy minimization: it absorbs fatty-acid monomers when they are abundant in the environment (active-state inference), excretes metabolic byproducts (sensory-state feedback), and adjusts its growth rate to match the available free energy in the environment (internal-state belief-updating).
The MITCHELL prediction: the dynamic Markov blanket of a Hadean alkaline-vent protocell IS computationally detectable in the Beck-Ramstead 2025 framework using only the macroscopic dynamics of the protocell-environment-coupled system, without requiring explicit knowledge of the protocell's molecular composition. This makes the Markov-blanket formalism deployable for remote biosignature detection: a putative living system on Europa or Enceladus need not be molecularly characterized in advance; its persistence dynamics, observed remotely or in-situ, are sufficient signature for a Markov-blanket-positive identification.
VIII.3 The 3C Convergence at the Markov-Blanket Layer
The Markov blanket IS the formal language in which causation, compartmentalization, and continuity converge:

The Markov blanket IS the compartmentalization condition (statistical isolation).

Part IX · The JWST K2-18b Biosignature Substrate and the Astrobiological Threshold
IX.1 The Madhusudhan 2023–2025 K2-18b Detection-and-Debate Cycle
K2-18b, a sub-Neptune exoplanet of mass 8.6 M_⊕ and radius 2.6 R_⊕ orbiting the M-dwarf K2-18 at 124 light-years distance, has been the most intensively-studied exoplanet biosignature target since 2019. Madhusudhan-Sarkar-Constantinou-Holmberg-Piette-Moses 2023 (Astrophys. J. Lett. 956, L13) reported a 5σ detection of CH₄ and 3σ detection of CO₂ in the H₂-rich atmosphere using JWST NIRSpec and NIRISS, consistent with a hycean-world scenario (planet-wide water ocean beneath an H₂-rich atmosphere). The same paper reported a low-significance (~1σ) tentative inference of dimethyl sulfide (DMS) — a molecule that on Earth is produced exclusively by marine phytoplankton.
Madhusudhan et al. 2025 (Astrophys. J. Lett., April 2025) extended the analysis to JWST MIRI 6–12 μm transmission spectroscopy and reported a 3σ inference of DMS and/or dimethyl disulfide (DMDS), strengthening the biosignature claim. The paper sparked an immediate detection-and-debate cycle:

Welbanks-Madhusudhan et al. 2025 questioned the statistical robustness of the DMS-DMDS inference.
Schmidt et al. 2025 reanalyzed the original 2023 NIR data with improved retrieval pipelines and confirmed CH₄ at 4σ but found no statistically significant evidence for CO₂ or DMS.
Stevenson et al. 2025 (arXiv:2508.05961) applied an "astrobiology standards of evidence" framework and concluded that K2-18b does not meet the operational threshold for biosignature evidence.
A&A Insufficient evidence for DMS and DMDS in the atmosphere of K2-18b (Aug 2025) presented a joint NIRISS+NIRSpec+MIRI analysis and found that red noise dominates the mid-IR data; no statistically significant evidence for biosignatures.
Madhusudhan et al. (continuing 2025–2026) maintained the 3σ inference and announced new MIRI observations to discriminate among DMS, DMDS, and abiotic alternatives.
